# BioNaural — Focus Categories to Explore

> **RESEARCH ARCHIVE:** This doc explored 13 focus categories across 4 tiers during the research phase. **The shipped product has 4 modes: Focus, Relaxation, Sleep, Energize.** This doc is retained for research context only — it informed the mode selection decision but does NOT represent the current feature set. See CLAUDE.md for authoritative mode definitions.

## Tier 1: Strong Evidence + High Demand (Launch Candidates)

### Deep Work
- **What:** Sustained analytical focus — coding, writing, data analysis, problem-solving
- **Frequency:** Beta 14-16 Hz
- **Evidence:** Beauchene et al. (2016, 2017) — improved working memory, frontal-parietal connectivity. Most replicated finding in the field.
- **Biometric behavior:** Maintain steady low HR, high HRV. If stress spikes (HR up, HRV down), shift toward alpha to calm without breaking focus.
- **Target audience:** Knowledge workers, programmers, writers

### Meditation
- **What:** Mindfulness, stillness, present-moment awareness
- **Frequency:** Theta 6-7 Hz
- **Evidence:** Strongest anxiety reduction evidence (meta-analytic d ≈ 0.45). Theta entrainment most consistently demonstrated on EEG. McConnell (2014): parasympathetic activation.
- **Biometric behavior:** Guide toward low HR, high HRV. Respond to restlessness (HR spikes) with deeper theta to re-center.
- **Target audience:** Meditation beginners, stress-prone users

### Workout
- **What:** Physical exercise — running, lifting, HIIT, cycling
- **Frequency:** Beta 18-25 Hz (embedded in rhythmic audio)
- **Evidence:** Weak for binaural beats alone. Strong for tempo-matched audio (Karageorghis, 2012: 10-15% endurance improvement). Best approach: embed beats in rhythmic audio.
- **Biometric behavior:** Match intensity. Rising HR → higher frequency, more energy. Falling HR → shift to recovery.
- **Target audience:** Fitness enthusiasts, gym-goers with Apple Watch

## Tier 2: Moderate Evidence + Clear Use Case (V1 or V2)

### Study / Learning
- **What:** Memorizing, reading textbooks, exam prep, language learning
- **Frequency:** Theta 6 Hz (encoding) → Beta 15 Hz (recall)
- **Evidence:** Memory tasks show most consistent meta-analytic results (Garcia-Argibay, 2019). Frontal midline theta linked to memory encoding (Jirakittayakorn, 2017).
- **Biometric behavior:** Detect fatigue (rising HR without exertion, declining HRV) and shift to alerting beta. During calm absorption, maintain theta for encoding.
- **Target audience:** Students, lifelong learners

### Recovery / Cool-Down
- **What:** Post-workout parasympathetic reactivation, winding down after intense activity
- **Frequency:** Theta 6 Hz
- **Evidence:** McConnell et al. (2014): theta beats post-exercise increased HF-HRV. Abeln et al. (2014): trends toward faster heart rate recovery. Most evidence-backed workout-adjacent application.
- **Biometric behavior:** Track HR recovery curve. As HR descends, deepen into theta/delta. Goal: accelerate return to resting state.
- **Target audience:** Athletes, fitness enthusiasts

### Sleep Preparation (Future — V2+)
- **What:** Pre-sleep wind-down, transitioning from wakefulness to sleep onset
- **Frequency:** Theta (4-7 Hz) → Delta (1-4 Hz) graduated over 20-30 min
- **Evidence:** Abeln et al. (2014): delta beats improved subjective sleep quality. Gao et al. (2014): delta increased slow-wave EEG consistent with sleep onset.
- **Biometric behavior:** Track HR and HRV settling. As body approaches sleep state, deepen from theta to delta. Session auto-ends when biometrics indicate sleep onset.
- **Target audience:** Insomnia sufferers, poor sleepers, shift workers

## Tier 3: Theoretical Support + Niche Appeal (Explore Later)

### Creative Focus
- **What:** Brainstorming, ideation, creative writing, visual art, music composition
- **Frequency:** Theta 6-7 Hz (divergent thinking) or Alpha 10 Hz (cognitive flexibility)
- **Evidence:** Theta correlated with creative insight (Razumnikova, 2007). Hommel et al. (2016): alpha promotes flexible processing. Individual differences are huge (Reedijk et al., 2013: dopamine moderates effects).
- **Biometric behavior:** Less prescriptive. Allow natural fluctuation. If user becomes too tense (HRV dropping), gently shift toward alpha/theta to open up.
- **Target audience:** Designers, writers, artists, musicians

### Reading / Comprehension
- **What:** Sustained reading — books, long articles, reports
- **Frequency:** Alpha 10-12 Hz
- **Evidence:** Theoretical. Maintains alert relaxation without narrowing attention (which could impair holistic text comprehension). No direct studies on binaural beats and reading.
- **Biometric behavior:** Similar to Deep Work but gentler. Maintain calm alertness. Detect drowsiness (HRV rising too high, HR dropping) and nudge toward low beta.
- **Target audience:** Readers, researchers, students

### Writing
- **What:** Long-form writing — essays, articles, fiction, journaling
- **Frequency:** Alpha-theta border 8-10 Hz
- **Evidence:** Largely speculative. The alpha-theta border is associated with the absorption state writers describe as "flow." No direct studies.
- **Biometric behavior:** Balance between relaxed ideation and enough focus to maintain coherence. Track whether user is stuck (stress rising without typing) vs. in flow (steady biometrics).
- **Target audience:** Writers, journalists, content creators

### Yoga / Mindful Movement
- **What:** Yoga, stretching, tai chi, walking meditation
- **Frequency:** Alpha 10 Hz → Theta 6 Hz as practice deepens
- **Evidence:** Theoretically aligned — alpha/theta matches brainwave states during meditative movement (Braboszcz et al., 2017). No rigorous outcome studies specific to binaural beats.
- **Biometric behavior:** Follow the body's rhythm. Active poses → maintain alpha. Restorative/savasana → deepen to theta. HR naturally guides this.
- **Target audience:** Yoga practitioners, mindful movement enthusiasts

### Pre-Performance / Psyching Up
- **What:** Pre-game, pre-presentation, pre-interview arousal optimization
- **Frequency:** Beta 18-25 Hz or low Gamma 30-35 Hz
- **Evidence:** Tod et al. (2003): psyching up improves force production 5-8% (d = 0.41). Extrapolated to binaural beats — not directly studied.
- **Biometric behavior:** Gradually elevate arousal. Track HR rising toward target zone. Once in optimal arousal range, maintain.
- **Target audience:** Athletes, performers, public speakers

## Tier 4: Speculative / Research Needed (Future Exploration)

### Monotonous Task Endurance
- **What:** Data entry, repetitive work, assembly, long drives
- **Frequency:** SMR 12-15 Hz
- **Evidence:** Drawn from neurofeedback/ADHD literature. Maintains arousal without overstimulation. No binaural beat studies.
- **Biometric behavior:** Detect drowsiness and nudge alertness up. Detect stress from boredom and keep engagement level.

### Social Focus
- **What:** Active listening, meetings, conversations, networking
- **Frequency:** Low beta 12-14 Hz (alert but not anxious)
- **Evidence:** None specific. Theoretical: maintaining calm alertness without anxiety during social interaction.
- **Note:** Wearing headphones during social interaction is a UX challenge. Bone conduction? One-ear mode?

### Emotional Regulation
- **What:** Managing anxiety, anger, overwhelm in real-time
- **Frequency:** Theta 6-7 Hz (calming) with biometric-driven intensity
- **Evidence:** Anxiety reduction is the strongest evidence area. HRV biofeedback for emotional regulation is well-supported (Lehrer & Gevirtz, 2014).
- **Note:** Approaches clinical claims. Must be positioned as wellness, not treatment.

### Lucid Dreaming Preparation
- **What:** Training the brain for awareness during sleep
- **Frequency:** Gamma 40 Hz during REM-adjacent states
- **Evidence:** Voss et al. (2014): gamma-frequency tACS during REM induced lucid awareness. Not binaural beats, but same frequency target. Highly speculative for consumer app.

## The Meta-Insight

The specific binaural beat frequency matters less than the adaptive engine. The research consistently shows:
1. Individual differences are enormous
2. Personalized approaches beat presets
3. Closed-loop (adaptive) beats open-loop (static)
4. The biometric feedback loop is more defensible than frequency claims

**BioNaural's categories should be defined by the user's intent (what are you doing?) and the biometric response (how is your body responding?), not by a fixed frequency prescription.** The frequency is a starting point. The adaptation is the product.
