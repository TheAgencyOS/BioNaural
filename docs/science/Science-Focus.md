# BioNaural — Binaural Beats & Focus Science

> What the research says about binaural beats for deep work, flow, creativity, study, ADHD, and cognitive fatigue.

---

## Deep Work / Sustained Attention

**Best supported frequency:** Low beta (14-16 Hz)

| Study | Journal/Year | Key Finding |
|-------|-------------|-------------|
| Beauchene et al. | *PLOS ONE*, 2016 | **15 Hz beta binaural beats improved visuospatial working memory** accuracy. EEG showed increased frontal-parietal connectivity. One of the most methodologically solid studies. |
| Beauchene et al. | *Experimental Brain Research*, 2017 | Confirmed: beta (15 Hz) strengthened long-range cortical connectivity for sustained attention. Theta (5 Hz) degraded working memory. |
| Kraus & Porubanová | *Studia Psychologica*, 2015 | Modest working memory improvements with beta beats. Effect size: d ≈ 0.3-0.4. |
| Colzato et al. | *Psychological Research*, 2017 | 40 Hz gamma narrowed attentional spotlight (increased local processing, reduced global/diffuse). |
| Garcia-Argibay et al. | *Psychological Research*, 2019 | Meta-analysis (22 studies): small but significant overall cognitive effect (g ≈ 0.32). Memory tasks most consistent. |

**Takeaway:** Low beta (14-16 Hz) is the most consistently supported range for sustained attention and working memory.

## Flow State

**Theoretical frequency:** Alpha-theta border (7-10 Hz) — but direct evidence is largely absent.

**What we know about flow's brainwave signature:**
- Transient hypofrontality — reduced prefrontal cortex activity (Dietrich, 2004). Loss of self-consciousness, time distortion.
- Alpha-theta crossover — theta power rises to meet/exceed alpha. First documented in neurofeedback by Peniston & Kulkosky (1989, 1990).
- Not a fixed frequency — it's a shifting pattern of alpha, theta, and intermittent low-gamma bursts.

| Study | Journal/Year | Finding |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Hommel et al. | *Frontiers in Psychology*, 2016 | Gamma (40 Hz) promoted focused/persistent cognitive style. Alpha (10 Hz) promoted flexible processing. Both are components of flow. |
| Reedijk et al. | *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience*, 2013 | **Critical finding:** Binaural beat effects depend on individual baseline dopamine levels (proxied by eye-blink rate). Low dopamine benefited from gamma; high dopamine did not. |

**No controlled studies have directly measured flow state (e.g., Flow State Scale) during binaural beat exposure.** Claims are extrapolated from the neurofeedback literature. The theoretical rationale (alpha-theta border stimulation supporting absorption) is plausible but unproven.

## Creative Focus

**Theoretical frequency:** Theta (5-7 Hz) for divergent thinking. Gamma (40 Hz) for convergent problem-solving.

| Study | Journal/Year | Finding |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Reedijk et al. | *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience*, 2013 | No main effect on creativity overall. BUT: significant interaction with dopamine — low-blink-rate individuals showed improved divergent thinking with gamma (40 Hz). |
| Hommel et al. | *Frontiers in Psychology*, 2016 | Alpha promotes **cognitive flexibility** (brainstorming). Gamma promotes **cognitive persistence** (analytical solving). Different creative tasks need different frequencies. |
| Chaieb et al. | *Frontiers in Psychiatry*, 2015 | Theta (5-7 Hz) has theoretical potential for creative ideation but empirical support is inconsistent. Small samples, no blinding. |

**Supporting neuroscience (not binaural-beat-specific):**
- Razumnikova (2007): increased frontal theta during creative problem-solving
- Lustenberger et al. (2015): theta-frequency brain stimulation (tACS, not binaural) enhanced creative thinking — confirms the causal role but used a different modality

**Takeaway:** Individual differences (especially dopamine levels) moderate effects substantially. One frequency won't work for everyone.

## Study / Learning / Memory

**Theoretical protocol:** Theta (6 Hz) during encoding, beta (15 Hz) during recall.

| Study | Journal/Year | Finding |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Garcia-Argibay et al. | *Psychological Research*, 2019 | Meta-analysis: **memory tasks showed the most consistent positive effects**. Long-term memory > short-term. Small-to-medium pooled effect size. |
| Jirakittayakorn & Wongsawat | *Frontiers in Neuroscience*, 2017 | 6 Hz binaural beats increased frontal midline theta (associated with memory encoding). Neurophysiological, not behavioral. |
| Gálvez et al. | *International Journal of Neural Systems*, 2018 | 10 Hz binaural beats improved verbal memory recall in Parkinson's patients — suggesting benefits for baseline cognitive deficits. |
| Kennerly | Dissertation, 1994 | Beta (14 Hz) improved reading comprehension in college students. Widely cited but never peer-reviewed. |
| Ortiz et al. | 2008 | Binaural beats during word-list learning: no significant recall improvement, but altered EEG. Small sample. |

**Underlying neuroscience:** Theta-gamma coupling in the hippocampus is a key memory encoding mechanism (Lisman & Jensen, 2013, *Trends in Cognitive Sciences*). Whether binaural beats can meaningfully influence this coupling is unproven.

## ADHD & Focus

**Not an evidence-based treatment.** Preliminary and suggestive at best.

| Study | Journal/Year | Finding |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Kennel et al. | *Journal of Pediatric Nursing*, 2010 | Pilot (n=20): parent-reported inattention improvements with beta/SMR (12-15 Hz) beats. No significant change on objective measures. Uncontrolled. |
| Shekar et al. | 2018 | 16 Hz and 40 Hz beats in adults with ADHD symptoms: some sustained attention improvement. Small, community (non-clinical) sample. |

**Frequencies used in ADHD research:** SMR (12-15 Hz) or low beta (14-20 Hz), targeting the deficit in beta-band power and excess theta typical in ADHD.

**Important distinction:** Active neurofeedback for ADHD has stronger evidence (Arns et al., 2009, meta-analysis). Binaural beats are passive — likely weaker effects. Should not replace established treatments.

## Cognitive Fatigue

| Study | Journal/Year | Finding |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Axelsen, Kirk & Bhatt | *PLOS ONE*, ~2020 | Binaural beats during prolonged cognitive task: **lower subjective fatigue** but objective performance decline was similar to controls. |
| Crespo et al. | *Neuroscience Letters*, 2013 | 5 Hz theta increased drowsiness markers. 10 Hz alpha maintained more alert EEG during prolonged performance. |

**Takeaway:** Alpha (10 Hz) or low beta (12-15 Hz) for combating fatigue. Avoid theta during long work — it promotes drowsiness. Evidence that binaural beats outperform other background audio for fatigue mitigation is weak to nonexistent.

## Task-Specific Frequency Map

The most practical synthesis from the research:

| Task Type | Suggested Frequency | Evidence Level | Rationale |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------|-----------|
| **Analytical/logical** (coding, math, data) | Beta 14-16 Hz | Moderate | Beauchene et al.: improved working memory, frontal-parietal connectivity |
| **Deep reading/comprehension** | Alpha 10-12 Hz | Low-moderate | Maintains alert relaxation without narrowing attention |
| **Creative brainstorming** | Theta 6-7 Hz or Alpha 10 Hz | Weak | Theta/alpha = divergent thinking and cognitive flexibility |
| **Creative problem-solving** | Gamma 40 Hz | Weak | Narrowed focus, improved convergent thinking (in some individuals) |
| **Memorizing / encoding** | Theta 6 Hz | Low-moderate | Frontal midline theta = memory encoding mechanism |
| **Active recall / test prep** | Beta 15 Hz | Moderate | Working memory improvements (Beauchene) |
| **Sustained monotonous work** | SMR 12-15 Hz | Low-moderate | Maintains arousal without overstimulation (neurofeedback/ADHD literature) |
| **Writing** | Alpha-theta border 8-10 Hz | Theoretical | Balances relaxed ideation with focus. Largely speculative. |

## Critical Caveats

1. **Effect sizes are small** (g ≈ 0.32 for cognition). Comparable to background music — real but modest.
2. **Individual differences are enormous.** Reedijk et al.'s finding that dopamine levels moderate effects is probably the most important finding in the field. A frequency that helps one person may be neutral or counterproductive for another.
3. **Entrainment varies wildly between individuals.** Some show robust frequency-following; others show minimal change (Gao et al., 2014).
4. **Expectation/placebo effects are large and poorly controlled.** Many studies lack proper active controls.
5. **No established dose-response curve.** Duration varies from 3 to 30+ minutes across studies.
6. **Binaural beats vs. isochronic tones:** A 2024 EEG study (Neuroscience, Elsevier) found isochronic tones produced significantly greater EEG power changes than binaural beats at gamma frequencies (p < 0.001). For Focus mode specifically (beta 14-16 Hz, gamma 40 Hz), isochronic tones may offer stronger cortical entrainment. ## What This Means for BioNaural

1. **Deep Work mode has the strongest scientific foundation** — beta 14-16 Hz for sustained attention is the best-supported use case.
2. **Study mode is defensible** — theta for encoding, beta for recall. Memory tasks show the most consistent meta-analytic results.
3. **Flow state claims should be avoided** — no direct evidence. But the adaptive engine (responding to biometrics) is the real differentiator, not fixed-frequency claims.
4. **Individual differences demand personalization** — this is BioNaural's core advantage. Static presets can't account for dopamine variation, baseline brainwave patterns, or personal response profiles.
5. **Minimum 15-minute sessions** — shorter sessions likely don't produce meaningful cognitive effects.
6. **Isochronic tones are the strongest fit for Focus mode** — higher frequencies (beta/gamma) favor isochronic's stronger cortical evoked response. 40 Hz gamma isochronic has the most research support for attention and working memory (Jirakittayakorn 2017, Colzato 2016/2017).
