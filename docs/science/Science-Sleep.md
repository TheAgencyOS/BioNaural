# BioNaural — Sleep Mode Science

> Delta waves, sleep onset, the headphone problem, and how to position sleep preparation.

---

## Delta Waves & Sleep

Delta oscillations (0.5-4 Hz) are the dominant EEG signature of N3/slow-wave sleep (SWS). Generated by thalamocortical circuits. SWS is critical for:

- **Memory consolidation** — hippocampal-to-neocortical transfer (Diekelmann & Born, 2010; Rasch & Born, 2013)
- **Glymphatic clearance** — brain waste removal increases ~60% during SWS (Xie et al., 2013, *Science*)
- **Growth hormone** — GH secretion peaks during first SWS episode (Van Cauter et al., 2000)
- **Immune function** — pro-inflammatory cytokine activity linked to SWS

Healthy adults: ~15-20% of total sleep in N3, concentrated in first 3 hours. Declines significantly with age.

## Key Studies

| Study | Year | Journal | N | Finding |
|-------|------|---------|---|---------|
| **Abeln et al.** | 2014 | *European J Sport Science* | 15 | Binaural beats + music increased SWS time and subjective sleep quality (actigraphy, not PSG) |
| **Lee et al.** | 2019 | *Complementary Therapies in Clinical Practice* | 15 | 3 Hz delta beats for 30 min nightly × 4 weeks: significant PSQI improvement vs. control |
| **Jirakittayakorn & Wongsawat** | 2017 | *Frontiers in Neuroscience* | 24 | 6 Hz theta beat increased theta EEG power, trend toward faster sleep onset (nap protocol) |
| **Garcia-Argibay et al.** | 2019 | *Psychological Research* | Meta | Small effect on anxiety (relevant to sleep onset), inconclusive for sleep specifically |
| **Ingendoh et al.** | 2023 | *Psychological Research* | Meta | Limited entrainment evidence. Possible relaxation/indirect effects. |

**Bottom line:** Evidence is suggestive but not definitive. The strongest pathway is **indirect** — binaural beats reduce pre-sleep anxiety/rumination → faster sleep onset, rather than directly entraining delta oscillations in a sleeping brain.

No rigorous PSG study has demonstrated binaural beats changing sleep architecture in a clinically meaningful way.

## Theta-to-Delta Transition Protocol

**The concept:** Start at theta (6-8 Hz) to match the brain's drowsy/hypnagogic state, gradually reduce to delta (1-3 Hz) to mirror the natural N1→N2→N3 transition.

**Evidence:** This is primarily a **commercial practice, not a studied protocol.** The rationale is drawn from EEG norms — natural sleep onset follows alpha→theta→delta progression. Matching and leading the brain's frequency is intuitively appealing but unproven.

**Recommended defaults for BioNaural:**

| Parameter | Default | Range |
|-----------|---------|-------|
| Starting frequency | 6 Hz (theta) | 6-10 Hz |
| Target frequency | 2 Hz (delta) | 1-3 Hz |
| Ramp duration | 25 minutes | 15-45 min |
| Ramp curve | Exponential decay (faster initial descent) | Linear or exponential |
| Carrier frequency | 150 Hz (warm, soothing) | 100-250 Hz |

## The Headphone Problem

Binaural beats require stereo headphones. Sleeping with headphones is uncomfortable, impractical, and potentially unsafe.

| Solution | Binaural Beats Work? | Practicality |
|----------|---------------------|-------------|
| AirPods / earbuds | Yes | Fall out, uncomfortable for side sleepers |
| **Sleep headphones (headband style)** | **Yes** | **Best option.** SleepPhones, Kokoon. Recommend to users. |
| Pillow speakers | No (usually mono, poor separation) | Comfortable but defeats binaural mechanism |
| Bone conduction | No (bypasses interaural processing) | Comfortable but binaural beats don't work |
| Regular over-ear headphones | Yes | Impractical for sleeping |

**Design implication:** BioNaural should support both:
- **Binaural beats** (for headphone users)
- **Isochronic beats** (amplitude-pulsed at target frequency, works through speakers/pillow speakers/bone conduction — no stereo required)

Detect audio output route and suggest the appropriate mode.

## Sleep Onset Detection & Auto-Stop

### With Apple Watch

| Signal | What It Tells You | Reliability |
|--------|------------------|-------------|
| **Motion (accelerometer)** | Sustained motionlessness = likely asleep | Best single signal. 5-10 min of no movement. |
| HR decline | HR drops at sleep onset (~5-10 BPM) | Good supporting signal, gradual |
| HRV increase | Parasympathetic tone rises at sleep onset | Good supporting signal |
| Respiratory rate | Stable, low rate during sleep | Not available as real-time API for third-party apps |

**Recommended trigger:** 10 minutes sustained low motion + HR below user's personal sleep threshold → begin 5-minute audio fade-out.

### Without Apple Watch
Simple timer with gradual 5-minute fade-out after user-set duration (default 30 min).

## Position as Sleep Preparation, NOT Through-the-Night

**Strong recommendation:** 15-45 minute pre-sleep sessions. NOT all-night audio.

**Why:**
1. No evidence that continuous binaural beats during sleep improve quality
2. Auditory stimuli during sleep can cause micro-arousals and fragment sleep architecture (Ngo et al., 2013)
3. Headphone comfort deteriorates over 8 hours
4. Battery drain: audio + Watch sensors + Bluetooth all night = 30-50% iPhone, 25-40% Watch
5. BioNaural complements Apple's native sleep tracking rather than competing with it

**User narrative:** "Use BioNaural for 20-30 minutes to prepare your brain for sleep, then let your body's natural sleep systems take over."

## Comparison with Other Sleep Audio

| Approach | Mechanism | Evidence | Headphones Required? |
|----------|-----------|---------|---------------------|
| **Binaural beats** | Entrainment + relaxation | Low-moderate | Yes (stereo) |
| White/pink noise | Sound masking | Low-moderate | No |
| Brown noise | Sound masking (bass-heavy) | Anecdotal only | No |
| ASMR | Parasympathetic activation (for responders) | Very low | No |
| Sleep stories (Calm) | Cognitive distraction | Very low (for stories specifically) | No |

**BioNaural opportunity:** Layer binaural/isochronic beats under ambient soundscapes (rain, ocean, pink noise) for combined masking + relaxation + potential entrainment. Hedges the bet — even if beats have minimal direct effect, the sound masking still provides value.

---

## Screen & Light Considerations

- **Dark/red UI during sleep mode.** Blue light (446-477 nm) suppresses melatonin via melanopsin receptors (Chang et al., 2015, *PNAS*).
- Auto-blank screen once session starts. Audio only — no visual engagement needed.
- Suggest enabling Night Shift.

## What This Means for BioNaural Sleep Mode

1. **Position honestly:** "Designed based on sleep neuroscience" — not "clinically proven to increase deep sleep"
2. **Theta→delta ramp** is the primary protocol (6→2 Hz over 25 min). Mirrors natural sleep onset.
3. **Support both binaural and isochronic beats.** Detect audio output and recommend the right mode.
4. **Sleep preparation only** (15-45 min). Auto-fade on sleep onset detection. Not through-the-night.
5. **Layer beats under ambient soundscapes.** Combined masking + relaxation + potential entrainment.
6. **Integrate Watch sleep data** for personalized outcome tracking over time.
7. **Dark/red screen.** Minimize blue light emission.
8. **Biometric-driven adaptation:** Watch HR/HRV dropping → beats deepen toward delta. Watch detects sleep → fade out.
