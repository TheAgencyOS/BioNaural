# BioNaural — The Neuroscience of Binaural Beats

> How binaural beats work, what the brain actually does, and where the science is solid vs. speculative.

---

## How Binaural Beats Work

Discovered by Heinrich Wilhelm Dove in 1839. When two pure tones of slightly different frequencies are played separately to each ear via headphones (e.g., 400 Hz left, 410 Hz right), the brain perceives a third "beat" at the difference frequency (10 Hz). This beat has no physical acoustic reality — it's generated entirely in the auditory brainstem, specifically at the **superior olivary complex (SOC)**, the first site where signals from both ears converge.

### The Neural Response

**Frequency Following Response (FFR):** Subcortical neurons in the brainstem fire in phase-locked synchrony to the beat frequency. First characterized by Moushegian, Rupert, and Stillman (1973). Smith, Marsh, and Brown (1975) confirmed it works specifically for binaural beats. The FFR is most robust below ~30 Hz and degrades significantly above that.

**Auditory Steady-State Response (ASSR):** A sustained evoked potential measurable via EEG. Has both subcortical and cortical generators. The 40 Hz ASSR is uniquely strong due to natural resonance of auditory cortex (Galambos, Makeig, and Talmachoff, 1981). However, binaural beat ASSRs are weaker than monaural beat ASSRs (Schwarz and Taylor, 2005).

### Perceptual Constraints
- Requires headphones (each ear must receive only one frequency)
- Carrier frequency difference must be < ~30-35 Hz (above this, two separate tones are heard)
- Carrier frequencies should be below ~1000 Hz (phase-locking degrades at higher frequencies)
- The perceived beat is faint — a gentle "wobbling" or pulsation (Licklider, Webster, and Hedlun, 1950)

## Does Entrainment Actually Work?

The central claim: the brain's dominant EEG frequency will synchronize to the binaural beat frequency. The evidence is mixed.

### Evidence FOR

| Study | Journal/Year | Finding |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Karino et al. | *J Neurophysiology*, 2006 | MEG showed cortical responses phase-locked to 4-6.66 Hz binaural beats in auditory cortices |
| Pratt et al. | *Clinical Neurophysiology*, 2010 | EEG showed increased spectral power at beat frequency, especially in frontal/parietal regions. Clearest for beta-range. |
| Gao et al. | *PLOS ONE*, 2014 | 40 Hz binaural beats modulated gamma-band activity and influenced working memory |
| Jirakittayakorn & Wongsawat | *Frontiers in Neuroscience*, 2017 | Increased theta EEG activity during 6 Hz binaural beat exposure |

### Evidence AGAINST

| Study | Journal/Year | Finding |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Orozco Perez, Borges & Vollenweider | *eNeuro*, 2020 | No consistent frequency-specific entrainment. ASSR was present but didn't propagate beyond auditory cortex. |
| Lopez-Caballero & Escera | *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience*, 2017 | Systematic review of 22 EEG studies: evidence for entrainment was "inconsistent" |
| Goodin et al. | *Frontiers in Psychiatry*, 2012 | No significant entrainment for theta/delta beats despite subjective changes |
| Vernon et al. | *Experimental Brain Research*, 2014 | Double-blind: no EEG changes from 5-minute binaural beat exposure |

### Key Meta-Analyses

| Study | Journal/Year | Finding |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Garcia-Argibay, Santed & Reales | *Psychological Research*, 2019 | 22 studies: small but significant effect on cognition (g ≈ 0.32). High heterogeneity. |
| Garcia-Argibay, Santed & Reales | *Annals of NY Academy of Sciences*, 2019 | Moderate effect on anxiety (d ≈ 0.45). Small samples noted. |
| Ingendoh, Posny & Heine | *Psychological Research*, 2023 | Updated meta-analysis: small positive effects on anxiety and some cognitive tasks |

### Bottom Line

The brain reliably produces a local auditory response at the beat frequency (ASSR/FFR). Whether this local response becomes **global cortical entrainment** that shifts cognitive state is not robustly supported. Behavioral effects (anxiety reduction, some cognitive modulation) are real but small, and may work through relaxation, focused attention, and expectation rather than pure frequency-specific entrainment.

## The Five Brainwave Bands

| Band | Range | Associated States | Key Science | Binaural Beat Evidence |
|------|-------|------------------|-------------|----------------------|
| **Delta** | 0.5-4 Hz | Deep sleep, unconsciousness, healing | Thalamocortical circuits. Memory consolidation (Rasch & Born, 2013). Growth hormone release. | Very few controlled studies. Plausible for sleep but poorly documented. Hard to deliver (must stay awake with headphones). |
| **Theta** | 4-8 Hz | Light meditation, creativity, drowsiness, memory encoding | Hippocampal theta critical for memory (Buzsaki, 2002). Frontal theta = creative insight (Razumnikova, 2007). Elevated in experienced meditators (Aftanas & Golocheikine, 2001). | **Most consistently demonstrated** ASSR effects. Best evidence for anxiety reduction and meditative states. |
| **Alpha** | 8-13 Hz | Relaxed wakefulness, calm alertness, default mode | First described by Berger (1929). Alpha inhibition hypothesis: suppresses task-irrelevant areas to aid attention (Klimesch et al., 2007). | Most studied band. Relaxation/anxiety results are positive but inconsistent on replication. |
| **Beta** | 13-30 Hz | Active thinking, concentration, alertness | Frontal beta = top-down cognitive control. Motor cortex beta = maintaining current state (Engel & Fries, 2010). High beta (>20 Hz) = anxiety. | Lane et al. (1998): 16-24 Hz increased alertness and vigilance task performance. |
| **Gamma** | 30-100 Hz | Peak cognition, sensory binding, consciousness, advanced meditation | Binding hypothesis (Singer & Gray, 1995). Remarkable gamma in long-term meditators (Lutz et al., 2004, PNAS). | Problematic: >30 Hz beat frequency pushes against where binaural beats produce coherent percepts. Some subcortical evidence (Becher et al., 2015). |

## Carrier Frequency: The Underappreciated Variable

The base tone matters more than most apps acknowledge.

| Carrier Range | Neural Response | Perceptual Quality | Source |
|--------------|----------------|-------------------|--------|
| ~200 Hz | Moderate | "Rougher," less pleasant | General finding |
| **~400 Hz** | **Strongest** | **Smooth, tolerable for extended listening** | Schwarz & Taylor (2005), Grose & Mamo (2012) |
| ~600 Hz | Moderate | Adequate | General finding |
| >1000 Hz | Weak/unreliable | Beat detection becomes unreliable | Grose & Mamo (2012) |

**Recommendation for BioNaural:** Carrier frequency around 400 Hz (±100 Hz) optimizes both neural response strength and listening comfort.

## Duration: How Long Before It Works?

| Duration | What Happens | Source |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| Instant | ASSR begins within 200-500ms — but this is just the auditory reflex | Ross et al. (2000) |
| < 5 min | No meaningful psychological effects | Vernon et al. (2014) — null results at 5 min |
| **10-15 min** | **Minimum threshold for behavioral effects** | Consensus across positive studies |
| **15-30 min** | **Most common duration in studies showing positive results** | Padmanabhan et al. (2005), Lane et al. (1998) |
| 30+ min | Deep relaxation/sleep protocols | Wahbeh et al. (2007) |
| Daily over weeks | Possible cumulative effects | McConnell et al. (2014) — 60 days daily |

**Implication for BioNaural:** Sessions should default to 15+ minutes. Shorter sessions may have limited effect.

## Risks & Contraindications

### Populations Requiring Caution

| Population | Risk | Severity |
|-----------|------|----------|
| **Epilepsy / seizure history** | Rhythmic stimulation can trigger seizures. No large studies on binaural beat seizure risk specifically, but precautionary principle applies. | High (caution) |
| Psychiatric conditions (psychosis, dissociative disorders) | Theta beats could theoretically exacerbate dissociation | Low-moderate |
| Children | No safety studies in pediatric populations | Unknown |
| Tinnitus / auditory processing disorders | Sustained tonal stimulation may worsen symptoms | Moderate |
| Driving / operating machinery | Delta/theta could promote drowsiness | Moderate |

### Common Adverse Effects (Reported)
- Headache with prolonged listening or high volume
- Dizziness/nausea (more common at higher beat frequencies)
- Paradoxical anxiety increase with beta-range beats (Lane et al., 1998)
- Dissociation/depersonalization with theta (anecdotal)

### For General Healthy Population
At reasonable volumes, binaural beats are essentially harmless acoustic stimuli. The primary risk is opportunity cost — using them as a substitute for evidence-based treatments.

## What This Means for BioNaural

1. **The science is real but modest.** Small, consistent effects — not transformative. Position accordingly.
2. **Theta and alpha have the strongest evidence.** Deep Work (alpha) and Meditation (theta) modes have the most scientific backing.
3. **Gamma via binaural beats is problematic.** >30 Hz beat frequencies don't produce reliable percepts. Workout mode may need to use beta-range instead or layer other audio approaches.
4. **400 Hz carrier frequency** is the sweet spot.
5. **15+ minute sessions** are the minimum for meaningful effects.
6. **Individual differences are enormous.** What works for one person may not work for another. This is the strongest argument for BioNaural's adaptive approach — personalization over presets.
7. **The adaptive biometric loop may be more defensible than the binaural beats themselves.** Biofeedback has stronger evidence than passive entrainment.
