# Science: Energize Mode

> Research compiled from 50-agent parallel investigation, April 2026.
> This document covers the neuroscience foundations for a 4th BioNaural mode targeting alertness and energy.

---

## Evidence Tier Assessment

**Honest ranking: Relaxation > Focus > Energize.**

The evidence for beta/gamma binaural beats increasing alertness is the weakest of the four modes. However, the *adaptive biometric component* has a stronger evidence base than the beats themselves — and that's where BioNaural's real claim lives.

---

## Beta Waves (14-30 Hz) and Alertness

Low beta (14-16 Hz) overlaps with Focus mode. **High beta (18-25 Hz)** is the Energize sweet spot — associated with general cortical activation, vigilance, and arousal.

- Garcia-Argibay et al. (2019, *Psychological Research*): Meta-analysis found small but statistically significant effects of binaural beats on attention (g = 0.32). Beta-range effects on vigilance are consistent but modest.
- Kennel et al. (2010, *Journal of Pediatric Nursing*): Beta binaural beats improved attention in ADHD children — small sample but directionally consistent.
- Colzato et al. (2017, *Frontiers in Human Neuroscience*): 40 Hz binaural beats enhanced attentional focus with self-reported wakefulness.

## Gamma Waves (30-50 Hz) and Arousal

40 Hz is the most studied gamma frequency, primarily due to MIT/Tsai lab Alzheimer's research.

- Iaccarino et al. (2016, *Nature*): 40 Hz light/sound entrainment activated microglia and reduced amyloid-beta in mice.
- Martorell et al. (2019, *Cell*): Combined 40 Hz auditory + visual stimulation showed broader brain effects.
- Chan et al. (2024, *PNAS Nexus*): Phase II human trial — 6 months of daily 40 Hz light+sound slowed brain atrophy in mild Alzheimer's.
- Jirakittayakorn & Wongsawat (2017, *Cognitive Neurodynamics*): 40 Hz binaural beats improved working memory and increased gamma EEG power.

**Caveat:** Most gamma research uses direct neural stimulation (tACS) or combined light+sound, not binaural beats alone. Audio-only gamma entrainment is less studied.

## Energize vs. Focus: The Neural Distinction

These are scientifically dissociable brain states:

| Dimension | Energized/Alert | Focused/Attentive |
|---|---|---|
| **Brainwaves** | Broad, undifferentiated beta | Structured theta-beta coupling, regional specificity |
| **Norepinephrine** | High tonic LC firing (scanning broadly) | Moderate tonic LC (locked on task) |
| **Dopamine** | Phasic bursts (novelty, reward-seeking) | Tonic PFC (goal maintenance) |
| **Cortisol** | Mildly elevated (arousal-promoting) | Low-moderate (prefrontal function preserved) |
| **Heart Rate** | Elevated | Moderate, stable |
| **HRV** | Lower (sympathetic dominance) | Higher (parasympathetic engagement) |
| **Subjective** | "Wired but scattered" — high energy, rapid thought-shifting | "Locked in" — sustained, narrow attention |

**Key insight:** You need *some* arousal to focus, but excess arousal without prefrontal regulation produces energized-but-scattered. The HRV signature differentiates them: focus requires parasympathetic co-activation, not just sympathetic drive.

## The Norepinephrine System

The locus coeruleus (LC) is the brain's master arousal dial:

- **Moderate NE** activates high-affinity alpha-2 adrenergic receptors in prefrontal cortex → enhanced working memory, focused attention
- **Excessive NE** recruits lower-affinity alpha-1 and beta receptors → impaired PFC function, amygdala reactivity, anxiety

The **Yerkes-Dodson curve** maps this: performance follows an inverted-U as a function of arousal. Simple tasks tolerate higher arousal; complex cognitive work requires staying closer to the moderate midpoint.

**Implication for Energize:** Target the moderate zone, not peak arousal. 16-22 Hz beta is the safe operating range.

## Dopamine and Audio Stimulation

- Salimpoor et al. (2011, *Nature Neuroscience*): Music triggers dopamine release in the striatum — caudate during anticipation, nucleus accumbens during peak pleasure.
- Dopamine response depends on musical prediction/resolution cycles — binaural beats alone lack the structural complexity to reliably trigger this.
- **Implication:** The melodic layer (not just beats) is essential for Energize. Binaural beats provide cortical arousal; the melodic layer provides dopaminergic motivation.

## Sympathetic Nervous System and Audio

Safe sympathetic activation through sound relies on:

- **Rhythmic entrainment** at escalating tempos (audiomotor synchronization)
- **Bass-frequency vestibular stimulation** (40-150 Hz activates the sacculus, bypassing cortical processing)
- **Groove-driven dopamine release** (syncopation and slight rhythmic deviations)
- **Predictability and consonance** (keeps the system in "challenge" state, not "threat" state)

The distinction between arousal and stress: **predictability and agency**. Energizing stimulation is rhythmically predictable, gradually escalating, and harmonically consonant. Stress-inducing sound is unpredictable, dissonant, and uncontrollable.

## Isochronic Tones: Potentially Superior for Energize

Isochronic tones may be more effective than binaural beats at beta/gamma frequencies:

- Schwarz & Taylor (2005): Amplitude-modulated tones produced larger auditory steady-state responses than binaural beats at the same frequency.
- Lopez-Caballero & Escera (2017): Monaural/isochronic stimulation produces more reliable frequency-following responses in beta range and above.
- Binaural beat perception degrades above ~30 Hz; isochronic tones face no such ceiling.
- Isochronic tones work through speakers (no headphones required).

**Recommendation:** Consider isochronic tones as the primary entrainment method for Energize, with binaural beats as a secondary/optional layer. A hybrid approach (binaural for subcortical, isochronic for cortical) is justified as an opt-in intensity tier.

## The Adaptive Engine as Scientific Differentiator

The strongest honest framing for Energize:

- Binaural beats for alertness: small, inconsistent effects (weakest of 4 modes)
- HRV biofeedback: large meta-analytic effect size (g = 0.81, Goessl et al., 2017)
- Adaptive always beats static in controlled studies
- **Lead with the adaptive system, present the frequencies as the mechanism it uses**

The closed-loop biometric response is what makes Energize defensible — not the beat frequencies alone.
