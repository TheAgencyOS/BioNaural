# Energize Mode: Use Cases & Target Demographics

> Maps when, why, and for whom the Energize mode exists.

---

## Primary Use Cases (Daily Anchors)

### 1. Morning Wake-Up (Sleep Inertia Clearing)
- **When:** 6-8 AM, first 20-30 min after waking
- **Biometrics:** Low HR (resting), high HRV (parasympathetic dominance)
- **Desired outcome:** Alert, clear-headed, ready to start the day
- **Session length:** 10-15 minutes
- **Protocol:** Theta (6 Hz) → alpha → beta (14-16 Hz) ramp matching natural EEG progression during healthy waking. Melodic alarm alternative (McFarlane et al., 2020: melodic alarms reduce sleep inertia vs harsh alarms)
- **Demand: HIGH** — near-universal need
- **Unique opportunity:** Closed-loop biometric-driven wake-up audio. Watch detects wake-up (accelerometer + HR spike), assesses inertia severity from prior HR depth, delivers personalized theta→beta ramp. **Nobody does this.**

### 2. Afternoon Slump Recovery
- **When:** 1-3 PM, post-lunch circadian trough
- **Biometrics:** Dropping HR, rising HRV (parasympathetic rebound), reduced movement
- **Desired outcome:** Regain alertness without caffeine or a nap
- **Session length:** 8-12 minutes
- **Protocol:** Alpha → high beta ramp. More aggressive stimulation needed during circadian troughs.
- **Detection:** HRV-to-HR ratio rising in early afternoon = real-time slump indicator
- **Demand: HIGH** — office/remote workers hit this wall daily

### 3. Pre-Meeting Energy Boost
- **When:** 5-10 min before a call or presentation
- **Biometrics:** Variable — flat HR from passive desk work, or elevated from stress
- **Desired outcome:** Sharpness, confidence, verbal fluency
- **Session length:** 3-5 minutes (must be quick)
- **Demand: MODERATE-HIGH** — frequent for knowledge workers

## Secondary Use Cases

### 4. Pre-Workout Priming
- **When:** Before gym/run, variable times
- **Biometrics:** Resting HR, moderate HRV, low recent activity
- **Desired outcome:** Mental activation, physiological readiness
- **Session length:** 5-8 minutes
- **Protocol:** Dual-channel priming — beta beats for cognitive focus + tempo-matched melodic content (120-130 BPM) for autonomic arousal
- **Biometric success markers:** HR increase 5-15 BPM, HRV decrease (sympathetic activation), LF/HF shift
- **Demand: MODERATE** — fitness-oriented subset, but highly engaged and consistent

### 5. Rest-to-Activity Transition
- **When:** After nap, long couch session, or extended reading
- **Biometrics:** Low HR, elevated HRV, minimal recent movement
- **Session length:** 5-10 minutes
- **Demand: MODERATE** — common on weekends

### 6. Post-Travel / Jet Lag Recovery
- **When:** Arrival day, misaligned circadian window
- **Biometrics:** Disrupted HRV patterns, irregular HR baseline, abnormal skin temperature rhythm
- **Desired outcome:** Override circadian drag, function in new time zone
- **Session length:** 15-20 minutes
- **Role:** Arousal bridge during re-entrainment (2-5 days). Complements light therapy.
- **Demand: LOW-MODERATE** — infrequent but high-value

### 7. Monday Morning / Post-Weekend Reset
- **When:** Monday 7-9 AM
- **Biometrics:** Weekend sleep drift (later bedtime), elevated morning HR
- **Session length:** 10-15 minutes
- **Demand: MODERATE** — biometrically a variant of morning wake-up

## The Complete Daily Cycle

Adding Energize fills the missing "rest-to-active" transition:

```
Wake (Energize) → Work (Focus) → Break (Relaxation) → Work (Focus) → 
Afternoon dip (Energize) → Evening (Relaxation) → Night (Sleep)
```

This creates **5-7 potential touchpoints per day** instead of 2-3, transforming BioNaural from a "work tool" to a "daily operating system for energy."

## Target Demographics

### Day-One Audience (Already in the Funnel)

**Remote Workers (Afternoon Motivation)**
- Strongest overlap with existing BioNaural users
- Lack office social/environmental cues to push through energy dips
- Already own Apple Watches and use focus tools

**People Reducing Caffeine**
- Health-conscious professionals predisposed to biometric alternatives
- $2.8B caffeine alternative market (2024), growing ~10% CAGR
- 42% of US adults actively tried to reduce caffeine in past year (IFIC 2024)

**ADHD Individuals**
- Cortical under-arousal drives stimulation-seeking (Optimal Stimulation Theory)
- Already gravitate toward binaural beats for focus
- Energize addresses the distinct need for activation/arousal
- **Safe framing:** "Designed for brains that crave stimulation" (experiential, not clinical)

### Growth Audience (New Users)

**New Parents (Sleep-Deprived)**
- Need functional energy, not deep focus — genuinely new use case
- High Apple Watch ownership (30-40 age range)
- Could bring users who wouldn't otherwise try BioNaural

**Frequent Travelers (Jet Lag)**
- Business travelers = knowledge workers with high spending power
- Energize positions BioNaural as a travel-recovery tool
- High Apple Watch adoption in this demographic

**Students (Exam Periods)**
- Long study sessions need energy + focus in combination
- Slightly lower Apple Watch penetration but meaningful overlap

### Lower Priority
- Shift workers (lower Apple Watch adoption, non-desk roles)
- Athletes (niche, sport-specific tools dominate)
